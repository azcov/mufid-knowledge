# Competitor Analysis — Invoice App

## Primary Competitor: Paper.id

| Dimension | Paper.id | Invoice App |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Target | SME → Enterprise | Freelancer → Small agency |
| Pricing | Freemium + Rp 99.667/mo subscription + transaction fees | Transaction fee only, no subscription |
| Complexity | High (corporate cards, expense mgmt, supplier payments) | Low (invoice → send → get paid) |
| Onboarding | Multi-step KYB for lower fees | Minimal — bank account number only, no documents |
| e-Meterai | ✓ (required for formal B2B) | Phase 2 |
| Indonesian payment methods | 30+ | Same via Xendit/Midtrans |

## Key Insight (from docs)
Paper.id went upmarket into a "full business financial OS."
That's their strength for enterprise. Their weakness: a solo designer doesn't need a corporate card.
**Our wedge: the freelancer experience Paper.id deliberately abandoned when they went upmarket.**

## What Paper.id Gets Wrong
- Complexity designed for finance teams, not individuals
- KYB documents required for best rates (barrier for informal freelancers)
- No emphasis on client payment experience beauty
- Trying to be everything = nobody's first choice for simple invoicing
