# Social Media Strategy

## Short Answer
**No — not every product needs its own social media account.**

Especially not now. Creating 12 accounts and posting on all of them is a full-time job. For a solo founder, scattered presence = wasted energy.

---

## Principles

### 1. Launch account only when product is live
No point building an audience for a product that isn't shipped yet.
Exception: pre-launch waitlist accounts — only if you're doing a proper pre-launch campaign.

### 2. One channel done well > five channels done poorly
Pick the right platform per product, post consistently, then expand.

### 3. Studio account first, product accounts later
Run **@mufidazizlabs** as the umbrella — post about all products there first. Spin off a dedicated account only when a product has enough traction to deserve it (500+ users, consistent MRR).

## Platform Priority by Product Type

### B2C Products (BudgetNest, DeenDaily, Kulitku, MenuQR)
| Platform | Why |
|----------|-----|
| **Instagram** | Visual, Indonesian young adults, high engagement |
| **TikTok** | Massive reach for how-to / before-after content |
| **Twitter/X** | Tech-adjacent users, good for DeenDaily Muslim community |

### B2B Products (SlipGaji, WaCRM, Bengkelterdekat, Kostify, IndoDev-ID)
| Platform | Why |
|----------|-----|
| **Facebook Groups** | Where Indonesian SME owners actually are |
| **LinkedIn** | For IndoDev-ID specifically (developer/company audience) |
| **Instagram** | Still works for Indonesian B2B at SME scale |

### Directory / SEO Products (Suratpedia, Bengkelterdekat, Sekolah-Dir)
- Social media is secondary — **SEO is the primary channel**
- Social only needed for link building and brand trust
- TikTok / Instagram Reels for discovery (e.g. "cara cari bengkel spesialis")

## Recommended Account Rollout

### Phase 0 — Now (nothing released)
| Account | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| `@mufidazizlabs` (Instagram + Twitter) | Studio brand, building in public, product updates |

Just one account. Post about what you're building. Build the founder audience, not the product audience.

### Phase 1 — First products go live (Q2–Q3 2026)
Spin off accounts for the first 2 products that launch:

| Product | Platform | Account |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Suratpedia | Instagram + TikTok | `@suratpedia` — template demos, tips |
| Bengkelterdekat | Instagram + Facebook | `@bengkelterdekat` — "bengkel spesialis" content |

### Phase 2 — More products live (Q3–Q4 2026)
| Product | Platform | Account |
|---------|----------|---------|
| SlipGaji | Instagram + Facebook Groups | `@slipgaji.id` — payroll tips, PPh 21 content |
| Kostify | Facebook Groups + Instagram | `@kostify.id` — landlord tips |
| BudgetNest | Instagram + TikTok | `@budgetnest.id` — couple finance content |

### Phase 3 — Hire growth person (Rp 30–50M MRR)
Let the marketing hire manage the social calendar. Don't do it yourself at this stage.

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Create all 12 accounts on day one — you'll abandon most of them
- ❌ Post the same content on all platforms — each platform has its own format
- ❌ Buy followers — Indonesian SME audiences are relationship-based, not number-based
- ❌ Run paid ads before organic content works — validate message organically first
- ❌ Neglect Facebook Groups — this is where Indonesian UMKM owners actually live
